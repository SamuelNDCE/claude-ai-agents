## 0. What KERAUNOS is

An electromagnetic (maglev) space-launch program by **Samuel Edwards / Perpetual Technologies**.
Three launchers, phased Moon → Earth → Mars:

| Phase | System | Body | One-line |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **SELENE** | Moon | Open-air maglev ring on the south-pole ridge. No tube needed (Moon = vacuum). Pods = **EOS**. |
| 2 | **BRONTE** | Earth | Through-mountain evacuated tube at Chimborazo, Ecuador. Pod/fairing-sled = **ASTRAPE**. |
| 3 | Vacuum fleet | Airless bodies | SELENE-class kits stamped on Phobos/Deimos/Ceres/Belt. The core bet. |
| 4 | **ARES** | Mars | Featherweight tube (atmo is 0.6% of Earth). Closes the return-trip problem. |
| 5 | BRONTE full | Earth | Extend Chimborazo to ~38 km (Mach 12.6), then the 1,000 km injector if economics prove out. |

Naming convention: Greek. BRONTE (thunder) → ASTRAPE (lightning, her sister). SELENE (moon) → EOS (dawn, her sister).

## 2. BRONTE — the frozen design (REV N, approved)

**Route:** one straight-then-bent line **through** Chimborazo, azimuth 095°, passing 0.5 km N of the summit.
Fitted to real SRTM 30 m terrain by automated DEM scan.

- **Phase 1 (build first): 14 km.** Portal 4,069 m (only at-grade start the terrain allows). El Arenal
  viaduct (A-frame piers 110–590 m) + 1.2 km crest bore + truss muzzle. Cargo Mach ~8.9 (30 g), 10 s in tube.
- **Full line: ~38 km.** West portal ~1,360 m (Chazojuán valleys), 24 km deep tunnel (cover ≤880 m),
  El Arenal viaduct, crest bore. Cargo Mach ~12.6 (30 g), human Mach ~4.6 (3 g), 16 s.
- **Both share the crest muzzle: 6,160 m**, exit 12.3° east, ~100 m below peak elevation (6,263 m).
  Terminal bend R = 100 km over final 11 km (the payload-g limit) lifts exit 6.5°→12.3°, +550 m.
- **Tube:** Ø4.0 m flight bore / Ø6.0 m ring-stiffened vacuum vessel / Ø12 m TBM bore. **360° stator ring**
  (24 REBCO sectors/ring, hoops every 0.6 m, ~63,000 on full line) — levitate+center+steer+propel
  (REQUIRED because the bend pushes the 15 t vehicle outward at up to 16 g). Vacuum isolation gates every
  500 m. 20 K + 80 K cryo headers full length, cooler stations every 500 m. Radial truss spokes brace the
  vessel 360° to the liner.
- **ASTRAPE vehicle:** 23 m, Ø3.6 m, 15 t baseline / **20 t max gross**. 14 m ogive nose (3.9:1) + 9 m
  cylinder. Payload bay **Ø3.2 m × 8 m** (Cygnus-module class). 2× V-tail micro-fins + ventral body flap
  (stow flush in-bore, deploy after exit). Full-hull reaction sleeve. Heat shield/needle for Earth re-entry.
- **Per shot:** 15 t → 16.5 MWh (P1) / 32.8 MWh (full), peak 12.4 / 17.5 GW, from trackside flywheel/SMES;
  grid sees only 30 MW × ~66 min recharge.
- **Time in tube (rest→exit):** 30 g = 16 s full / 10 s P1; 5 g = 39 s (M6.0) / 24 s (M3.6); 3 g = 50 s / 31 s.

**Honest open issues (on the sheet):** El Arenal piers >Millau's 343 m record; plasma window only proven
at cm-scale (Ø4 m = 2.8× baseline power, gets first R&D $); the whole massif is a protected fauna reserve
under Ecuador's rights-of-nature constitution (maybe the hardest part); Mach-12 exits are loud regardless.

**Geology/site facts (from research agent):** andesite core is TBM-proven (Coca Codo Sinclair: 24.8 km,
9 m TBMs); volcano dormant-but-active (last eruption ~550 CE); Pallatanga fault (1797 Riobamba M~7.6) sets
~0.4 g seismic basis → at-grade/A-frame design; avoid SE Riobamba debris-avalanche deposit.

## 3. SELENE — current design (REV D, still iterating)

- **Site: Shackleton–de Gerlache Connecting Ridge, ~89.7°S** (confirmed best, see §4). Real LOLA map.
- **Two machines, one ridge:**
  - **EOS-1 (first build):** pod Ø1.2 m × 7 m, 500 kg, bore Ø1.6 m, gap 200 mm. Mission one = **cargo
    return to Earth**. 0.39 MWh/shot, peak 1.17 GW, recharge 2.4 min @ 10 MW, ~25 shots/hr → ~300 t/day.
  - **EOS-2 (second machine, future):** pod Ø2.0 m, ~2.5 t, bore Ø2.4 m, own pad on same ridge. Bigger
    cargo + satellite dispensers. 1.96 MWh, 5.8 GW, ~5/hr.
  - **Key insight:** at a fixed 10 MW solar plant **both throw ~300 t/day** — throughput is set by the
    power plant, not the pod. EOS-2 buys box size, not mass.
- **EOS-1 pod (REV D):** **heat shield at FRONT** (doubles as launch dust dome — one blunt face: dust at
  launch, plasma at Earth; flies shield-first whole trip). **Correction ENGINE at REAR** (Δv 0.77 km/s,
  Isp 350) + RCS quads, no fins. Full-hull reaction sleeve. Interchangeable payload racks.
- **Ring:** same 24-sector 360° REBCO ring as BRONTE but **open to vacuum** (no vessel, no gates, no
  plasma window). Sealed 20 K cryostats. **Sun shield wraps ~280°**, polished reflective foil both faces
  (underside rejects regolith ground-bounce albedo ~0.12 + surface IR). Radiators on shaded back.
- **Track:** raised on a **regolith embankment**, ~2.0° grade, muzzle +100 m (Panel C side profile, 1:1).
  Angle is for terrain/dust clearance, NOT physics (vacuum). Legs land on attached berm. Panel C embankment
  now drawn as engineered fill: compacted lift layers, original-grade datum, angle-of-repose (~32°) end face
  (no vertical cliff) ending in a toe.
- **Track ladder:** 2.9 km @ 100 g (escape 2.38 km/s in 2.4 s) → 12 km @ 30 g → ~50 km @ 3 g passenger.
- **Energy:** 0.41 kWh/kg orbit, 0.78 escape, 1.25 Mars transfer (vs Earth-to-LEO 8.9).

## 4. Key decisions / physics locked this session

- **Curves are forbidden at speed:** 20° bend at 4 km/s needs R~200 km. High-speed track is straight;
  only a gentle payload-g-limited terminal bend is allowed. This shaped the entire BRONTE route.
- **SELENE site = polar ridge, CONFIRMED.** Samuel challenged "months-long seasonal extremes" → answered:
  lunar axial tilt is only 1.54°, so the Moon barely has seasons. The ±130°C month-scale swings are the
  EQUATOR's 14-day-night problem. Polar ridges are the most thermally STABLE ground on the Moon (sun
  circles the horizon like a lighthouse). Worth it for: near-continuous solar, stable thermals for 20 K
  magnets, cold traps/ice next door, Earth always on horizon for comms, all orbit planes monthly.
- **One model across all views** — no stylized variants (learned the hard way on ASTRAPE).
- **Wide bore is cheap in rock, expensive at muzzle** (plasma window power ∝ bore area).
