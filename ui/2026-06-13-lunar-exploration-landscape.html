The headline: Artemis got torn up and rebuilt (early 2026)

SLS upgrades cancelled

Feb 27, 2026

  • Block 1B + Exploration Upper Stage scrapped
  • Later flights move to a commercial upper stage (reported: ULA Centaur V)
  • FY2027 budget calls SLS "grossly expensive and delayed"

Gateway cancelled

Mar 24, 2026

  • Lunar orbit station dropped "in its current form"
  • Redirected to a $20B surface base
  • Leaves ESA / JAXA / Canada / UAE partners in limbo

Artemis III redefined

No longer the first landing

  • Now a LEO crewed docking test with the landers — no landing
  • Target NET late 2027
  • Crew: Bresnik, Parmitano (ESA), Douglas, Rubio

Artemis II flew ✓

Apr 1–11, 2026

  • First humans beyond LEO since 1972
  • Wiseman, Glover, Koch, Hansen (CSA)
  • Orion heat-shield issue resolved — "significantly reduced" char loss

Two blocs racing for the south pole

🇺🇸 Artemis Accords

USA-led · 52 signatories (2026)

Norms for civil lunar activity. Commercial-heavy: NASA buys rides & landers from industry.

Members incl. ESA states, Japan, India, South Korea, UAE, Canada, Australia, Brazil…

🇨🇳 ILRS

China + Russia · ~17 countries

International Lunar Research Station — a state-led Moon base. Basic model ~2035, expanded ~2045.

Members incl. Russia, Pakistan, Venezuela, South Africa, Egypt, Belarus, Thailand, Nicaragua, Serbia, Kazakhstan, Senegal…

The players

NASA core

Artemis · Administrator Jared Isaacman

  • $8.5B Artemis systems in FY2027 (topline cut ~23%, science −47%)
  • 3-phase $20B base roadmap (2026 → 2032 permanent)
  • "MoonFall" hopping drones + simplified LTV
  • China framing drives the urgency

SpaceX HLS

Starship HLS · Artemis III & IV

  • $2.89B (2021) + ~$1.15B (2022) HLS contracts
  • Critical path: ~10 tanker launches + orbital refueling (demo NET late 2026, unproven)
  • Flight 12 (May 22 2026, first V3): booster crashed, Raptor 3 issues
  • OIG: "well behind schedule," elevator/tip-over risks

Blue Origin HLS

Blue Moon MK1 / MK2 · BE-7

  • MK1 cargo (~3t) built; New Glenn exploded May 28 2026 → MK1 NET late 2026
  • MK2 crewed (~$3.4B, Artemis V) needs Cislunar Transporter refueling
  • National Team: Lockheed, Draper, Boeing, Astrobotic, Honeybee
  • 2nd MK1 delivers VIPER (~$190M, late 2027)

China CNSA leader

Chang'e + crewed + ILRS

  • Chang'e 6: first farside sample return (2024)
  • Chang'e 7: south-pole water-ice hunt + crater "hopper" (H2 2026)
  • Chang'e 8: 3D-print bricks from regolith (~2028)
  • Long March 10 hop test + Mengzhou abort test (Feb 2026)

Firefly Aerospace CLPS ✓ landed

Blue Ghost

  • M1 landed Mare Crisium Mar 2 2025 — first full commercial landing (2025 Collier Trophy)
  • M2 farside late 2026 (deploys ESA Lunar Pathfinder)
  • M3 Gruithuisen Domes 2028; M4 south pole 2029

Intuitive Machines CLPS tipped

Nova-C · NSN comms

  • IM-1 (2024) & IM-2 (2025) both reached surface but tipped over
  • IM-3 in 2026 (Reiner Gamma, 3 rovers)
  • Near Space Network comms: up to $4.82B
  • Dropped from the LTV down-select (May 2026)

Astrobotic CLPS M1 failed

Peregrine / Griffin

  • Peregrine failed (Jan 2024, tank rupture)
  • Griffin-1 now NET July 2026 (Falcon Heavy) w/ CubeRover
  • Lost VIPER delivery when NASA cancelled it

ispace Japan/Lux 0-for-2

Hakuto-R / APEX / ULTRA

  • M1 (2023) & M2 "Resilience" (Jun 2025) both crashed
  • M3 = Draper CP-12 farside (Schrödinger), → 2027
  • New "ULTRA" lander consolidates designs (Mar 2026)

Lunar Outpost + Astrolab LTV winners

Crewed rovers · May 2026

  • Lunar Outpost Pegasus (~$220M) + Venturi Astrolab FLEX/FLIP (~$219M)
  • Blue Moon MK1 delivers them (~$234M/mission)
  • LTV program up to $4.6B / 15 yrs; Astrolab has a Starship ride booked

ESA Europe

Argonaut · Moonlight · ESM

  • Argonaut cargo lander (~2030–31, budget pressure)
  • Moonlight comms/nav constellation (precursor ops 2026)
  • European Service Module powers every Orion
  • Parmitano = first ESA astronaut on Artemis

ISRO / JAXA India + Japan

Chandrayaan · SLIM · LUPEX

  • India: Chandrayaan-3 landed 2023; -4 sample return ~2028; human on Moon by 2040
  • Japan: SLIM precision landing (2024); Toyota "Lunar Cruiser" pressurized rover
  • Joint LUPEX polar rover (~2027–28)

Lunar economy startups

  • Crescent (Lockheed) / IM NSN: comms + PNT
  • Lonestar: Moon data centers · Nokia: lunar 4G
