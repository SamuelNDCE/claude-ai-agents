The tunnel sheet, sized from the payload outward: your Ø3 m+ requirement becomes a Ø3.2 m × 8 m payload bay (a Cygnus-class station module fits with margin) inside the Ø3.6 m ASTRAPE-H heavy vehicle, riding a Ø5.0 m flight bore in a Ø6.0 m ring-stiffened steel vacuum tube, all inside a Ø12.0 m single-pass TBM bore — smaller than Seattle's Bertha (17.45 m), bigger than Ecuador's own Coca Codo Sinclair machines (9.04 m), squarely within world practice. Counterintuitive physics that helps you: in a vacuum tube, blockage ratio barely matters, so the bore is set by guidance dynamics, not aerodynamics — and the tube wall is sized by external crush (buckling), not internal pressure, which is what the stiffening rings every 2.5 m are for. The honest cost of width is at the muzzle: plasma window power scales with area, so Ø5.0 m is ~4.3× the Ø2.4 m baseline. Wide is cheap in the rock and expensive at the exit. Drive numbers scale accordingly: 4.4 MN of thrust on the 15 t vehicle, 16–33 MWh per shot delivered at up to ~18 GW from trackside flywheel/SMES banks, with the grid only ever seeing a gentle sub-hour recharge.
