The Rocket Equation (Tsiolkovsky)

Core Formula
Δv = Isp × g₀ × ln(m₀ / m_f)
m_ratio = m₀/m_f = e^(Δv / (Isp × g₀))
propellant_fraction = 1 - 1/m_ratio

m₀ = wet mass (full). m_f = dry mass (empty). ln = natural log.

Rearranged for mass ratio: how much of the rocket must be propellant.

Orbital Mechanics Fundamentals

Kepler's Three Laws

1. Ellipses: Every orbit is an ellipse with the central body at one focus.

2. Equal areas: A line from body to orbiting object sweeps equal areas in equal time. (Angular momentum is conserved — faster at periapsis, slower at apoapsis.)

3. Period law: T² ∝ a³

T = 2π × √(a³ / μ)

a = semi-major axis (km). μ = gravitational parameter. T in seconds.

Vis-Viva Equation — velocity anywhere in an orbit

v = √( μ × (2/r − 1/a) )
Orbit typeConditionResult
Circularr = av = √(μ/r)
Ellipticalr variesuse full eq.
Parabolic (escape)a → ∞v = √(2μ/r)
Hyperbolice > 1v > escape

This one equation gives velocity at ANY point in ANY orbit. Most-used formula in astrodynamics.

Transfer Orbits

Hohmann Transfer (most efficient two-burn)

Δv₁ = √(μ/r₁) × [√(2r₂/(r₁+r₂)) − 1]
Δv₂ = √(μ/r₂) × [1 − √(2r₁/(r₁+r₂))]
T_transfer = π × √((r₁+r₂)³ / (8μ))

r₁ = departure orbit radius (km). r₂ = arrival orbit radius. μ = body's gravitational parameter. Hohmann is optimal only when r₂/r₁ ≤ 11.94.

Bi-Elliptic Transfer

More efficient than Hohmann when r₂/r₁ > 11.94 (very large orbit changes). Uses 3 burns via a high intermediate apoapsis.

Δv_total = Δv₁ + Δv_int + Δv₂

Gravity Assist (Swingby)

Fly close to a body and steal orbital energy from it. No fuel burned for the speed change.

Δv_gain = 2 v_body × sin(δ/2)

δ = deflection angle. v_body = planet velocity. Voyager used Jupiter + Saturn assists.

Free Return Trajectory

A TLI trajectory where if no LOI burn occurs, the spacecraft returns to Earth naturally (Apollo 13). Adds ~5–10 m/s Δv cost vs optimum but is a safety standard.

Keraunos Lunar Mass Driver — Trajectory Calculations

Keraunos on the Moon

The lunar mass driver is the Moon-side complement to Keraunos Earth. On the Moon: no atmosphere, 1/6 gravity, 2.38 km/s escape velocity. A mass driver ~1–5 km long can achieve full escape velocity.

v_exit = √(2 × a_g × L)

v_exit = exit velocity. a_g = acceleration (g). L = track length (m).

L_required = v²_exit / (2 × a_g)

Min track length for target velocity at given acceleration.

t_accel = v_exit / a_g = √(2L / a_g)

Time pod spends on track. Short = high g-force spike.

Bottom line for Project Kronos: Earth → Moon costs ~15.3 km/s total Δv. Moon → Earth costs only ~2.7 km/s (aerobrake is free). Every kg of ISRU propellant produced on the Moon eliminates the need to launch that kg from Earth (at $1,000–3,000/kg Starship rates). At 15.3 km/s required and Isp 380s, the mass ratio from Earth surface to lunar surface is e^(15,300/3,727) = 59.5 — meaning 59 kg of propellant to deliver 1 kg to the lunar surface. ISRU + Keraunos inverts this economics entirely.
