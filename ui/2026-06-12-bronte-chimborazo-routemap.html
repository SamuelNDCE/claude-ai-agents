DETAILS

REV G — the exit is now where you wanted it: the bend starts 11 km out (R = 100 km, the payload-g limit), lifting the line ~615 m above the old 6.5° grade so the rock portal breaks out right at the ridge crest at 6,140 m, with a short truss carrying the muzzle to 6,255 m — eight meters below the summit's own elevation, half a kilometer north of the peak. Above your 6,000 m floor with room to spare; 6,500 m doesn't exist on this mountain without raising the whole line another 250+ m of pier height. The costs are printed, not hidden: viaduct piers reach ~590 m, and cargo gives up ~2 Mach (now ~12.6) because the bend caps at 16 g sideways — humans feel 2.2 g. Phase 1 is unchanged in spirit: 14 km from the only at-grade portal, riding the same bend to the same crest muzzle at Mach ~8.9. The vehicle is reborn as ASTRAPE (lightning — Bronte's mythological sister): a lifting-body fairing-sled with the skid embedded flush in the belly, nothing jettisoned, an X-59-style needle nose, and four all-moving micro-fins for stability and climb-rate trim. And the plasma window finally gets a plain-English panel: an invisible magnetically-held sheet of hot plasma that keeps air out of the vacuum tube while solid objects fly straight through — proven at centimeter scale, unproven at 2.4 m, which is exactly why phase 1 exists.
