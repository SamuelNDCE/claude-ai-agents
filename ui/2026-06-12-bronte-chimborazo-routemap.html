REV F — same alignment, now with the terminal up-bend you asked for and both build iterations stacked. The bend is as much as the physics sells: R = 143 km over the final 5 km buys +2.0° (exit 8.4°) and 87 m of lift, capped by the 15.8 g normal load on Mach-14.8 cargo (humans at 3 g feel just 1.6 g). The drive coasts through the bend so loads don't stack. Rock portal 5,694 m on the east face, truss muzzle at 5,754 m, about 0.9 km northeast of the summit. A genuine above-the-peak exit needs the whole line raised ~500 m — shown as the gray ghost on Panel B and rejected as baseline because it pushes viaduct piers to ~780 m and leaves phase 1 with no ground-level start. Phase 1 is 13 km because that's where the line touches the ground — start there, fire at Mach ~8.8, and when it's time to grow, keep boring west on the same line until it's the 38 km Mach-15 machine. The sled grew an X-59-style needle nose and chined forebody for shock-shaping at these speeds; the lawn chair keeps its seat.
