The big three stories today

German court: Google's AI Overviews are Google's own words, and Google is liable for false answers landmark

A landmark ruling found AI summaries are fundamentally different from conventional search: a search engine points to websites, but AI Overviews generate "independent, new, and substantive statements" only Google can verify. Ars Technica's framing: this "could spell doom for AI search industry." Separately, Google was ordered to put clearer links in AI search and let UK publishers opt out.
