01 · The Physics

Breaking the rocket equation

The rocket equation is brutal because propellant must lift itself. KERAUNOS deletes that term entirely: the accelerator never leaves the ground, so the pod is 80% cargo, 20% fuel — the exact inverse of a chemical rocket.

E = ½v² = 32 MJ/kg8.9 kWh per kilogram at 8 km/s.
At $0.06/kWh that is $0.53/kg of energy to orbital velocity. Everything above that is amortization, not physics.
a = v² / 2L = 3.3gA 1,000 km tube reaches 8 km/s at just 3.3g (0.8g at 4 km/s). StarTram's 130 km design needed a brutal 30g, cargo only. Length is the unlock.
Δv kick ≈ 100s of m/sGround launch alone is sub-orbital. A small restartable kick motor circularizes at apogee — the only propellant on board.
Aero-shell

The Breakaway Shroud

The pod rides inside a sacrificial hypersonic shell shaped with NASA X-59 shockwave-tailoring data. It eats the Mach 12 tube-to-air transition, then jettisons at 30–40 km, shedding dead thermal mass before the orbital burn. Triple-redundant cold-gas thrusters guarantee separation.

Exit geometry

The Mountain Muzzle

The tube exits at 4,000–6,000 m altitude on a mountain face. Air density at 5 km is roughly half sea level — halving peak drag and heating during the worst milliseconds of flight. Non-negotiable at Mach 12+.

04 · Engineering Systems

A machine with no throwaway parts

Not even an aborted launch is wasted — its kinetic energy flows back into the storage rings.

Vacuum interface

The MHD Plasma Window

A physical door at a Mach 12 muzzle would destroy itself. Instead: a magnetically confined plug of ionized gas — solid wall to the atmosphere, transparent to the pod. Real, patented tech (Hershcovitch, Brookhaven, 1995; holds ~9 atm). Scaling it from cm-class apertures to pod diameter is the flagship R&D work package; blast shutters stand behind it as the physical backup.

Energy

The Superconducting Power Tap

Regenerative braking returns an aborted pod's energy to the rings. Varistor quench protection sustains 100+ cycles/day. Between launches the track is a grid-scale battery — soaking surplus solar and wind, selling stability. It earns even when nothing flies.

Corridor defense

The Laser Broom

The launch power plant doubles as a debris-sweeping laser array, nudging junk off the corridor minutes before exit, while AI-planned kick burns route pods around high-risk clusters. High cadence requires a clean highway.

Circular economy

48-Hour Shell Recovery

Jettisoned aero-shells fall on hyper-predictable tracks. Autonomous sea drones catch them, re-coat the transpiration layer, and return them to the tube within 48 hours. The shell "sweats" liquid nitrogen through porous ceramics to survive 2,000°C+.
