VERY EARLY CONCEPT ILLUSTRATION — NOT A DESIGN DOCUMENT. This sheet is a rough first mock-up produced solely to give a visual sense of how the BRONTE Chimborazo demonstrator tube might sit on the mountain. No dimensions, grades, component counts, or construction details shown here have been engineered or validated. Everything will change significantly as real design work begins. This image exists to make the Chimborazo concept easier to picture — nothing more.

BRONTE-D concept: a 10–20 km class evacuated maglev tube climbing Chimborazo's western flank from a ~3,950 m entry portal on the páramo to a truss-mounted muzzle ~207 m above the 6,263 m Whymper summit. The sled fires east (prograde) to bank the +465 m/s equatorial rotation bonus; an optional west-fire test mode trades that bonus for an open-ocean recovery corridor over the Pacific. Exit air at 6,470 m is roughly 52% of sea-level density (45% of sea-level pressure), which is what halves peak drag and stagnation heating versus a ground-level exit.
