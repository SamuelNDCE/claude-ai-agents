VERY EARLY CONCEPT ILLUSTRATION — NOT A DESIGN DOCUMENT. This sheet is a rough mock-up produced solely to give a visual sense of how the BRONTE Chimborazo demonstrator tube might sit on the mountain. The terrain profile is real (SRTM 90 m elevation data along 1.469°S through the summit), but the track alignment, dimensions, and components are speculative and unvalidated. Everything will change as real design work begins.

BRONTE-D concept on the real mountain: SRTM 90 m terrain along 1.469°S shows the actual shape — rolling páramo at ~3,600–3,950 m out to 10+ km west, the gently rising El Arenal plateau (~4,000–4,900 m), then the steep glaciated cone gaining ~1,300 m in the last 3 km. The tube runs ~16.7 km: a surface-laid run across El Arenal (viaducts over the quebrada dips), a steep west-face climb, and a truss-mounted muzzle ~207 m above the 6,263 m Whymper summit, firing east (prograde) for the +465 m/s rotation bonus. Note 4 flags the real engineering catch this profile makes obvious: the high-speed end of the track wants to be straight, so the vertical curve has to happen early, while the sled is still slow — detailed alignment optimization is open work.
