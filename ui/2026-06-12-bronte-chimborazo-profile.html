The track alignment, dimensions, and components are speculative and unvalidated. Everything will change as real design work begins.

REV C, at true proportions: Chimborazo is a broad, gentle shield — 40 km across with only ~2.7 km of relief — so the launch tube is mostly a straight 10 km bore at a constant 25° through the rock under El Arenal, not a surface ramp. The 25° angle is chosen from the permitted 20–50° envelope because it matches the mountain's natural ~24° west face: the tube daylights at ~4,930 m, runs a short surface gallery up the face, and exits from a truss 207 m above the summit, firing east at Mach ~7.7 (30 g cargo mode; Mach ~2.4 human-rated at 3 g; a +4 km stretch bore reaches Mach ~9). Shallower than 25° would demand ~450 m towers on the cone; steeper just buries the entry station deeper for zero speed gain. Gravity loss along the bore is sin 25° = 0.42 g — noise against the 30 g drive.
